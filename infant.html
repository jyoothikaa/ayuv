DIARRHEA

According to Ayurveda, diarrhea occurs when the digestive fire is weakened, usually by excess pitta. Absorption and assimilation slow, and the movement of food through the digestive system is accelerated—causing liquidy stools.Baby's diarrhea is often due to altered flora in the gastrointestinal tract. The remedy is to pacify pitta and to gently kindle agni. The following strategies specifically counter pitta’s hot, sharp, liquid qualities and can be very helpful in supporting the return of normal bowel movements.

Solutions-

  • Stewed Apples-A baby's diarrhea can usually be corrected simply by feeding the chila some cooked apple. Remove the skin and seeds, cook the apple, and add 1/2 teaspoon ghee, a pinch of cardamom, and a small pinch of ginger. Stir it up well, and cool to room temperature.
  • Cooked Rice with Yogurt- Start with one cup of cooked basmati rice. Add 1 tablespoon of ghee and 1/4 cup plain, fresh (ideally homemade) yogurt. Mix together and enjoy. This dish is soothing, grounding, and nutritive, and the probiotics in the yogurt help to restore the strength of agni.
  • Poppyseed Porridge- You can also make your child a porridge of poppy seeds. Bring 2 cup milk and ,2 cup water to a boil, then add about 1 teaspoon poppy seeds. The seeds will swell and become soft; they will make a nice, easily digested porridge. It is a good food that helps to stop diarrhea. As a bonus, your child will also sleep soundly.

RESPIRATORY ISSUES

The most common respiratory issues for infants are upper respiratory infections caused by viruses. Cold symptoms in infants typically include runny nose, sneezing, congestion, cough and fussiness. These viral infections are treated with supportive care including nasal saline, a cool mist humidifier and rest.

Solutions-

  • Nasal Care: Instilling a few drops of warm ghee or sesame oil in the nostrils (Nasya) can help keep the respiratory passages clear and prevent infections.
  • Oil Massage (Abhyanga): Regular oil massage using warm sesame or coconut oil can improve circulation and promote relaxation. It also helps in reducing Vata imbalances. It is recommended to perform Abhyanga on the chest and back gently before bathing with warm water.
  • Hydration: Encourage your child to drink warm water throughout the day when sick. Herbal teas or medicated water infused with herbs like ginger or Tulsi can be beneficial.

EAR INFECTION

Babies and children are more prone to ear infections as their Eustachian passages are shorter and narrower that than those of adults. This not only makes it easier for bacteria to reach the middle ear, but it means fluid is more easily trapped. Because babies and children do not have a fully-developed immune system, it can be harder for them to fight off infections.

Solutions-

  • Vachalasunadi Coconut Oil is an Ayurvedic herbal oil that helps with ear issues, including infection and external damage. It’s utilised for ear drops and head massages.
  • Bala Oil 10ml or Bala Oil SoftGel Capsules naturally nourishes and improve voice quality. It supports different ailments like pain in ears, cataracts,gulma, headaches, and colic. It also helps in balancing the Vata and pitta doshas.
  • Onion: Onions can be used in many different ways to treat earaches. You could either juice an onion and put a few drops of the same in the ear or slice an onion in half and heat it before placing it over the ear like an ear muff. Onion contains anti inflammatory and antibacterial properties that help soothe earaches effectively and quickly.

COUGH

Viruses are germs that cause most of the coughs by infecting the nasal passage and causing congestion and mucus building. They are easily passed on from kids to kids through sneezing, coughing, being in close contact with other infected kids

Solutions-

  • Rasaparpati :1 g = 4 doses. To be given one dose at a time twice daily either with honey or with breast milk. Can be used safely.
  • Mulethi-You can take it in the form of Mulethi water or kadha. Just take 1 teaspoon of Mulethi powder and add to 1 glass of warm water. Drink this twice a day. Even though your kids may not prefer it much you can also add other spices and make a kadha.
  • Tulsi-Tulsi is referred to as "The Queen of Herbs" and "Mother Medicine of Nature" in Ayurveda. Put few tulsi leaves in purified or clean water and heat it for 10-15 minutes.Make your baby to drink this after cooling it down.

COLD

A cold is a contagious viral disease that infects the soft lining of the nose. It is a condition that can cure within a week or ten days. But if the symptoms are more serious it may lead to influenza. Cold is most common during the winter season and affects children soon.Sharing of things in between children may also lead to cold. Hand-to-hand contact is also one of the reasons for spreading cold.

Solutions-

  • Take some honey (shahad) and add a pinch of turmeric (haldi) and make the child eat 1 tablespoon of this.
  • Roast about 2 tablespoons of ajwain (carom seeds) in a dry pan for a few minutes till the seeds let out their menthol-like aroma. Take a square shape muslin cloth, put ajwain (carom seeds) on it and carefully tie it into a nice potli and use it as a compress or just keep it next to your baby's nose while she/he is sleeping.
  • Make a paste out of garlic (lehsun), ajwain (carom seeds) & onion (pyaj) and warm it on the gas. When it is lukewarm apply it on the chest, back & forehead of the child and make the child rest.

Fever simply means high body temperatureabove 37.5 C. Most fevers are caused by infections or other illnesses. Body fight with infection, bacteria or viruses by fever. Ayurvedic fever is described as Jwara roga. It is an imbalance between Kapha and Vata Dosha that causes developmental fever.High levels of Kapha in the body lead to cold and excess Vata reduces the digestive fire. There are thirteen types of common fever ( Jwar) in Ayurveda.

  • Cut an onion into slices and rub 2 – 3 slices on your child’s feet for 2 minutes each
  • Sponge the body and forehead with cool water.

PNEMONIA

According to Ayurveda, an imbalance in the three doshas (Vata, Pitta, and Kapha) can contribute to the development of diseases like pneumonia. Imbalances in the Kapha dosha, which governs the respiratory system, can make newborns more susceptible to respiratory infections. Ayurvedic practices aim to restore the balance of the doshas to support overall health.

Solutions-

  • Tulsi (Holy Basil) Tea: Tulsi leaves have antimicrobial properties and help relieve respiratory congestion. Boil a few leaves in water, strain, and drink this tea two to three times a day.
  • Turmeric Milk: Turmeric contains a compound called curcumin, which has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Mix half a teaspoon of turmeric powder in a glass of warm milk and drink it before bedtime.
  • Herbal Steam Inhalation: Boil water in a pot, add a few drops of eucalyptus oil or tulsi leaves, and inhale the steam. This helps clear congestion and ease breathing.

JAUNDICE

Jaundice is described as yellowing of the skin texture, the whiteness of the eyes, dark urine, followed by itchiness. It occurs when there is an accumulation of bilirubin, a yellow pigment produced during the breakdown of red blood cells. Jaundice is relatively common in newborns

Solutions-

  • Sunlight Exposure (Phototherapy): Exposure to natural sunlight is considered beneficial in Ayurveda for managing jaundice. Early morning sunlight is often recommended.
  • Massage with Herbal Oils: Gentle massage with herbal oils is a common practice in Ayurveda. Certain oils, such as coconut or sesame oil, might be used.
  • Bilirubin-Lowering Foods: Some Ayurvedic practitioners recommend a diet that includes foods believed to help lower bilirubin levels. This may include radishes, tomatoes, and leafy green vegetables.

GASTROENTERITIS

Gastroenteritis in infants, often referred to as stomach flu or stomach infection, is a condition characterized by inflammation of the stomach and intestines. It is commonly caused by viral or bacterial infections, and occasionally by parasites.it can be caused through viral infections,bacterial infections and parasitic infections.

Solutions-

  • Ginger:It has antibacterial,anti-inflammatory,antiviral and anti-oxidant properities.It will help in treating stomach infections and prevent watery stools,nausea,vomitings and abdominal cramps. Boil a tea spoon of ginger in a cup of water and cover it for 10-15 mins.Strain and add some honey and give it for 2 times a day to infants.
  • Asafetida:Endowed with antiviral and antibacterial properities,it is a good remedy for stomach flu.It helps to improve digestion.Just add a half tbsp of Asafetida to luke warm water and let baby consume 2 times a day.
  • Barley Water: Add Barley seeds to luke warm water and leave it aside for 30mins and let babies consume those barley water.
