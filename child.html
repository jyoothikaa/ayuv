BRONCHITIS

The main symptom of bronchitis is chronic inflammation of the bronchial tubes or the windpipes that carry air to the lungs. This infection causes profuse secretion of phlegm. Other notable symptoms are chest pain, chronic cough, fever, poor digestion and loss of appetite, and shortness of breath.Children,those with an impaired immune system are extremely prone to bronchitis. Shringyadi churna, kaphaketu, kaphachintamani, sitopaladi churna, pravala pisthi, rasa sindur, talisadi churna, haridra khand and vasarishta are a few effective Ayurvedic medicines for bronchitis.

Solutions-

  • Haldi or turmeric, commonly used Ayurvedic spices in the world, is the most common home remedy for bronchitis. One can take a teaspoonful of haldi mixed in a glass of milk in the night for relief. Haldi is more effective if taken on an empty stomach.
  • One can also mix equal quantities of dried ginger, black pepper and long pepper with honey and take a teaspoonful thrice every day.
  • Tulsi or holy basil is a great cure for bronchitis. Water boiled with tulsi leaves brings instant relief to a sore throat. One can use this water for gargling too.

INFLUENZA

Influenza is a viral infection and contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza virus that attacks on respiratory system – Nose, throat and lungs.In Ayurveda influenza is compared with sannipataj jwara (three doshas vata, pitta, kapha) and the loss of ojas ( immunity).

Solutions-

  • Make a turmeric paste:At the first sign of the flu, simply mix together equally parts raw honey and turmeric powder. Take 1 teaspoon of the paste every few hours.To make the formula more potent, mix 16 parts turmeric to 1 part black pepper and make a paste with raw honey.
  • Burn off toxins with ginger tea:steep three to five slices of fresh ginger in hot water. Note: Anyone with ulcers, inflammatory skin conditions, or bleeding disorders should avoid ginger tea.
  • Ashwagandha: This herb has been shown to reduce stress and boost the immune system.An adaptogen, it helps restore the endurance, energy, and stamina we need to fight the flu.Take 500 milligrams of the whole root after meals.

CONJUCTIVITIES

Conjunctivitis is known by the more common (and descriptive) name “pink eye.” The telltale signs of pink eye include redness, discharge, itchiness, and swelling in one or both eyes. There are multiple causes, but the contagious type of conjunctivitis is caused by a bacteria or virus that gets into the eye.

Solutions-

  • Soak half a teaspoon of Triphala powder in a glass of water overnight. Filter the water in the morning and use it to wash the eyes.
  • Mix some turmeric with a little amount of water, dip a small and neat piece of cloth in it and let it dry. Use this cloth to wipe the affected area of eyes to help reduce infection.
  • Steep a teaspoonful of coriander seeds in a cup of water for 15 minutes. Strain it and apply the water externally on the eyelids for relief from conjunctivitis.

COMMON COLD

A cold is a contagious viral disease that infects the soft lining of the nose. It is a condition that can cure within a week or ten days. But if the symptoms are more serious it may lead to influenza. Cold is most common during the winter season and affects children soon.Sharing of things in between children may also lead to cold. Hand-to-hand contact is also one of the reasons for spreading cold.

Solutions-

  • Take some honey (shahad) and add a pinch of turmeric (haldi) and make the child eat 1 tablespoon of this.
  • Roast about 2 tablespoons of ajwain (carom seeds) in a dry pan for a few minutes till the seeds let out their menthol-like aroma. Take a square shape muslin cloth, put ajwain (carom seeds) on it and carefully tie it into a nice potli and use it as a compress or just keep it next to your baby's nose while she/he is sleeping.
  • Make a paste out of garlic (lehsun), ajwain (carom seeds) & onion (pyaj) and warm it on the gas. When it is lukewarm apply it on the chest, back & forehead of the child and make the child rest.

SORE THROAT

A sore throat is an illness that hurts and irritates the throat, frequently making it feel scratchy. Children often suffer from it, and the symptoms frequently get worse while eating or drinking. It can be caused by many pathogenic viruses and bacteria. The group A streptococcus, which causes strep throat, is extremely infectious and spreads through contact with infected people or respiratory droplets.

Solutions-

  • Drinking warm water has a range of advantages, according to Ayurveda. It aids in the control of digestion and media (fat).It increases blood circulation, which makes you more attentive and reduces stress if you drink warm water while working.
  • Turmeric is a golden spice frequently advised in Ayurveda for a wide range of illnesses, from lowering swelling or inflammation to treating the common cold.
  • One of the main ingredients in herbal cough syrups is dry ginger, which is also known by the names soith, sukku, or soonth. When combined with honey, sonth is a comforting treatment for sore throat.
