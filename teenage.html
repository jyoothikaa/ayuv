MALARIA

Malaria is one of the most common yet serious ailments, especially during the monsoon season. It is caused by a bite of an Anopheles mosquito that infects you with parasites that can easily travel through the bloodstream disrupting the functions of various organs in the body. These parasites, once they enter the bloodstream, can spread the infection to the red blood cells and multiply within 48 to 72 hours.

Solutions-

  • Hed or Hartaki: Haritaki is grown in the Himalayas. Its therapeutic properties help in inhibiting parasite growth. Take three grams of haritaki powder with a glass of warm water and drink daily.
  • Giloy is an ayurvedic herb and it is packed with antioxidants that help the body fight disease causing free radicals. Taking 5-10 ml of giloy juice every day increases the count of blood platelets, boosting the hemoglobin and giving your body strength to fight the infection.
  • Dhaniya Paani: Consuming dhaniya ka paani is helpful in curing the fever and cold that one may experience during malaria.Boil 10 grams of fresh coriander leaves in 500 ml of water and then strain the leaves. Drink this water every day.

Dengue fever is not contagious, which means one person cannot pass it directly to another.

  • Mulethi For Fever Control:Mulethi helps balance the aggravated doshas and reduce inflammation. So, drinking herbal tea made from mulethi helps in reducing fever, inflammation and improves immunity. You can also use mulethi with sunthi and drink it twice a day.

HIV

HIV is a virus. It attacks the body’s immune system, and if left untreated can lead to AIDS. There is currently no effective cure, but proper medical care can help control HIV.You can get HIV by coming into direct contact with body fluids from a person who is HIV positive and who has a detectable viral load. The HIV in these fluids gets into the bloodstream of an HIV-negative person through the mucous membrane of the rectum/mouth/vagina/anus through cuts or sores, or by a syringe.
